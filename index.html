Problems with USB device

USB port is damaged

One of the most common fears when a USB drive cannot be opened is that the external drive itself is damaged. However, it is much more common for the error to come from the device to which the flash drive has been connected, and more specifically, from the USB port, which acts as a connector between the removable media and the computer. If the chosen USB port does not work, the content of the USB logically cannot be identified or modified.Therefore, before delving into the complex error causes and possible solutions below, first check that your USB drive is not connected to a faulty port. To do this, simply try other ports, more than one if necessary. If the drive recognition works then you know the problem lies with the USB port.

USB drive is corrupted

If the previous cases have been ruled out, perhaps we should resign ourselves to the possibility that the flash drive is damaged and that is the reason why it does not show up on the computer. At best it could be a loose contact piece, which can be compensated for simply by carefully connecting the flash drive. On the other hand, if the hardware of the unit is damaged, in principle there is no other choice than to take the flash drive to a professional to repair it or, at least, try to save the saved files.

The USB controller is missing the appropriate or most current drivers

USB connections are always managed, on the computer side, by called USB controllers. These drivers need the most current drivers to be able to recognize different components such as the mouse, keyboard or, as in this case, USB drives.Yes, indeed, is the lack of these drivers the cause of not showing the USB, the problem can sometimes be solved installing them.

Steps to follow (Windows):
In Windows, drivers can be updated using “Device Manager” and following these steps:
1. Type the combination [Windows] + [R] to open the search field “Run2.
2. Enter devmgmt.msc and confirm the search by clicking “OK”.
3. Double-click on the “Universal Serial Bus Controllers” option and right-click the unrecognized flash drive (usually marked with a red exclamation mark).
4. Click “Update Driver” and select “Search automatically for updated driver software”.

Steps to follow (Mac)
1. Open the Apple menu with the apple icon.
2. Select the "System Preferences" option.
3. Click “Software Update” and then “Update Now” if updates are available.
4. In older versions of macOS (up to High Sierra) the process is slightly different: in the Apple menu, select the App Store option and then the "Updates" menu.
5. Then macOS will automatically check for software updates. If available, install them by clicking “Update All”.
